Red Fort,Delhi

The Red Fort, located in the heart of Delhi, is a grand symbol of India’s rich history and Mughal architecture. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century, this massive red sandstone fortress served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. Its imposing walls, intricate marble inlays, and beautiful gardens reflect the artistic excellence of the Mughal era. Today, the Red Fort stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a powerful national symbol, where India’s Prime Minister hoists the tricolor every Independence Day. Explore the majestic Mughal architecture and stroll through its grand courtyards. Attend the evening light and sound show that brings history to life.

Taj Mahal,Agra

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most celebrated symbols of love and architectural brilliance in the world. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. Its perfect symmetry, intricate carvings, and the way it reflects changing hues with daylight make it a masterpiece of craftsmanship. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj Mahal stands as a timeless reminder of love, art, and history. Take a sunrise tour to see the marble glow in golden hues. Capture timeless photos by the reflecting pool and gardens.

Qutub Minar,Delhi

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a magnificent example of Indo-Islamic architecture and one of India’s tallest stone towers. Built in the 12th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and later completed by his successors, it stands over 72 meters tall. Made of red sandstone and marble, the tower is adorned with intricate carvings and Quranic inscriptions. Surrounding it are several historical structures, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Qutub Minar reflects the grandeur of early Delhi Sultanate craftsmanship and history. Explore the ancient Qutub complex and admire its intricate carvings. Learn about Delhi’s early Sultanate history through guided walks.

Rumi Darwaza,Lucknow

The Rumi Darwaza, located in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, is an iconic example of Awadhi architectural grandeur. Built in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula, it served as a magnificent gateway between the Bara Imambara and the city. Standing 60 feet tall, the structure features intricate floral designs and a massive arched entrance inspired by Persian and Mughal styles. Often called the “Turkish Gate,” its name reflects influences from Istanbul’s architecture. The Rumi Darwaza remains a proud symbol of Lucknow’s rich cultural heritage and artistic elegance. Walk through the grand gateway and experience Lucknow’s Nawabi charm. Stroll nearby markets for Awadhi crafts and local delicacies.

Ajanta Caves,Maharashtra

The Ajanta Caves, located in Maharashtra, are a stunning collection of rock-cut Buddhist monuments dating back to the 2nd century BCE. Carved into a horseshoe-shaped cliff along the Waghora River, the caves feature exquisite sculptures, murals, and paintings depicting the life of the Buddha and various Jataka tales. Renowned for their artistic mastery and spiritual depth, these caves beautifully blend architecture and devotion. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Ajanta Caves stand as a timeless testament to India’s ancient art, culture, and religious harmony. Discover ancient Buddhist art and frescoes that narrate sacred tales. Trek along the scenic Waghora River valley for breathtaking views.

Golden Temple,Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine of Sikhism located in Amritsar, Punjab. Built in the 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, it symbolizes equality, humility, and spiritual peace. The temple’s stunning golden façade, surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar (pool of nectar), creates a serene and divine atmosphere. Open to people of all faiths, it embodies the Sikh principle of universal brotherhood. The Golden Temple stands as a shining beacon of faith, service, and harmony. Experience peace while walking the Parikrama around the holy sarovar. Join the Langar to share a humble meal in a spirit of equality and devotion.
